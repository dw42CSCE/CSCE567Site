Exploring Patterns in Violence and Poverty

This interactive dashboard examines the complex relationships between poverty rates, firearm ownership, and homicide rates across the United States between 1997 and 2016. Open the visualization in fullscreen for easier viewing. To do so, scroll to the bottom of the visualization and click the button to the left of the share icon.

Violence and Poverty

This visualization explores the relationship between poverty rates and gun-related homicides in U.S. states from 1997 to 2016. Look for patterns between states with consistently high poverty and violence, or see how economic improvements may correlate with changes in homicide rates. The first page shows national averages of Homicide Rate and Poverty over time. The second has two color-coded maps showing the Homicide Rate and Poverty Rate by state at the year selected on the slider in the top right. The third and final page shows a map showing homicide rate in the year selected by the slider, but clicking on a state (shift-click to select multiple) will show the poverty rate and homicide rate for that state(s) over time.

Violence and Gun Ownership

This chart examines the connection between firearm ownership and gun-related homicides across states. Use it to identify whether higher rates of gun ownership align with increased violence, or explore how trends shift over time by region or policy changes. The first page shows national averages of Homicide Rate and Gun Ownership over time. The second has two color-coded maps showing the Homicide Rate and the Percentage of Households with firearms by state at the year selected on the slider in the top right. The third and final page shows a map showing homicide rate in the year selected by the slider, but clicking on a state (shift-click to select multiple) will show the Gun Ownership and homicide rate by type (Firearm or No Firearm) for that state(s) over time.

About This Project

This dashboard explores state-level trends in gun ownership, poverty, and homicide rates over time, allowing viewers to compare states, identify potential correlations, and reflect on patterns that emerge across decades. Users can explore line charts, color-coded maps, and area plots, all of which update interactively based on year selection and state filtering.

Use the top navigation or scroll to view the dashboard. All visualizations are hosted via Tableau Public and embedded directly in this site for easy access without additional libraries or downloads.
